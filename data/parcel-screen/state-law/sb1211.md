# SB-1211 — Increased Detached ADUs on Multi-Family Lots

- **Bill**: SB 1211 (Skinner, 2024)
- **Code sections**: Amends Gov Code §65852.2 (ADU statute)
- **Effective**: January 1, 2025
- **Source**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240SB1211
- **last_verified**: 2026-05-11

## Summary
Raises the cap on **detached** accessory dwelling units (ADUs) permitted on multi-family lots from **2 to 8**. Also clarifies ADU rules for conversion of non-livable space on MF properties. Materially increases unit density for existing MF properties without requiring rezone or subdivision.

## Eligibility Checklist

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Zone**: Parcel is in a multi-family residential zone (R-2, R-3, R-4, RM, RH, etc.) OR mixed-use zone permitting MF
- [ ] **Existing residential use**: Parcel has existing multi-family residential structure(s) — confirm via improvement value > $0 and zone consistency

### [VERIFY]
- [ ] **Existing unit count**: Count of existing legal residential units (assessor's typeuse code may indicate but verify)
- [ ] **Setback / dimensional feasibility**: Detached ADUs require physical space; verify lot dimensions support up to 8 detached units alongside existing
- [ ] **Local ADU ordinance**: Verify city has updated its ADU ordinance to reflect the 2→8 detached cap
- [ ] **Utilities and infrastructure**: Adding 8 detached ADUs may trigger utility upgrade requirements at the lot

## Yield Math
- Existing units (unchanged)
- **PLUS up to 8 detached ADUs** (was 2 before SB-1211)
- **PLUS** ADUs converted from existing non-livable space on the MF property (e.g., storage, basements, garages) — at least 1 per 4 existing units OR 25% of existing unit count, whichever is greater
- **Example**: 4-unit apartment property → 4 existing + up to 8 detached ADUs + 1 conversion ADU = **13 units total**
- Stacks with State ADU law for size and approval standards

## Caveats
- "Detached" means a separate structure, not internal or attached. Attached/internal ADU counts are not raised by SB-1211 (those follow general State ADU law).
- Local cannot impose minimum lot size that would prevent reaching at least some detached ADUs
- Local cannot require replacement parking (general ADU rule)
- Local objective standards apply (size, height, setbacks per State ADU law)
- ADU size: detached ADU up to 1,200 sqft (general) — local can be more permissive but not more restrictive
- This is a high-leverage law for **existing MF property acquisitions** where added units can be approved ministerially with no entitlement risk

## Local Implementation
- Has the city updated its ADU ordinance to the 8-detached cap?
- Are there local objective design standards beyond state minimums?
- Application fees and processing track for MF property ADUs
