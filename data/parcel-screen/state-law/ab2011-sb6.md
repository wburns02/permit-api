# AB-2011 + SB-6 — Housing on Commercial Corridors

- **Bills**: AB 2011 (Wicks, 2022) — Affordable Housing & High Road Jobs Act; SB 6 (Caballero, 2022) — Middle Class Housing Act
- **Code sections**: Gov Code §65912.100 et seq. (AB-2011); §65852.24 (SB-6)
- **Effective**: July 1, 2023
- **Sources**:
  - https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202120220AB2011
  - https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202120220SB6
- **last_verified**: 2026-05-11

## Summary
Permits **housing development on parcels zoned commercial, office, retail, or parking** along qualifying corridors — by-right ministerial approval, with no rezone required.

- **AB-2011**: 100% affordable OR mixed-income housing on commercial corridor sites, ministerial. Prevailing wage required; skilled & trained workforce required for projects ≥50 units.
- **SB-6**: Any income mix (market-rate allowed) on commercial sites, ministerial. Prevailing wage + skilled & trained workforce required.

Both allow conversion of underutilized retail/office/parking to housing without going through rezone, GP amendment, or discretionary entitlement.

## Eligibility Checklist (Common to Both)

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Zone**: Parcel is zoned commercial, office, retail, or parking (and permits NON-RESIDENTIAL uses by-right)
- [ ] **Corridor**: Parcel abuts a qualifying corridor or is along one
  - Corridor must be ≥70 ft right-of-way for "urban infill"
  - ≥100 ft for "suburban"
- [ ] **Not industrial**: Parcel is not zoned heavy industrial; not adjacent to active heavy industrial use
- [ ] **Not on Tribal/Sacred land**, not a hazardous waste site
- [ ] **Not in Fire Hazard Zone** (SRA/VHFHSZ) without mitigation
- [ ] **Not in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area** without mitigation

### [VERIFY]
- [ ] **Prior use was not housing demolition** of rent-restricted, tenant-occupied, or Ellis-Act-withdrawn housing in past 10 years
- [ ] **No existing housing on parcel** being displaced (or, if so, full replacement + relocation per state law)
- [ ] **Prevailing wage commitment** (both bills)
- [ ] **Skilled & trained workforce commitment** (AB-2011 ≥50 units; SB-6 always)
- [ ] **Affordability commitment**: AB-2011 requires 8% very-low + 5% extremely-low income OR 100% lower-income (varies by project type); SB-6 has no affordability requirement
- [ ] **Local implementing ordinance**

## Density / Yield Math
Density floors are statutory:
- **AB-2011 Urban tier**: ≥30 du/ac minimum allowed
- **AB-2011 Suburban tier**: ≥20 du/ac minimum allowed
- **AB-2011 Smaller jurisdictions**: ≥15 du/ac
- **SB-6**: density allowed by the most recent applicable Housing Element or required by zone, whichever is greater

Yield = du/ac × acres, by tier. **Stacks with Density Bonus Law** — see [[density-bonus]] — can push effective density significantly higher.

Local cannot impose:
- Lower density than the statutory floor
- Height limits below 35-65 ft (varies by tier)
- FAR below 0.6 to 3.0 (varies by tier)
- Parking requirements (zero parking allowed within 0.5 mi of transit)

## Caveats
- **Labor cost premium**: Prevailing wage + skilled & trained workforce significantly raises hard costs. Pencils best on larger projects (≥50 units) where the cost is amortized.
- **Affordability premium (AB-2011)**: 100% affordable or 8%/5% mixed-income compresses revenue. Density bonus stacking is critical to math.
- **Corridor definition is technical**: Verify against city's adopted corridor map or do GIS spatial analysis against ROW width
- **Recent legislation**: AB-1893 (2024) and AB-1287 (2023) further amended density bonus interplay — verify current

## Local Implementation
- Most cities still adapting; some have not published official corridor maps
- Verify the city's procedure for AB-2011/SB-6 applications (likely a streamlined ministerial path)

## When Worth Considering
Most relevant for:
- Underutilized commercial strip retail along arterials
- Parking lots adjacent to active retail
- Office parks in declining submarkets
- Older auto-oriented commercial nodes near transit
