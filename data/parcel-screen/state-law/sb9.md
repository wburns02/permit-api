# SB-9 — Urban Lot Split + Duplex on R-1

- **Bill**: SB 9 (Atkins, 2021)
- **Code sections**: Gov Code §65852.21 (two-unit projects), §66411.7 (urban lot splits)
- **Effective**: January 1, 2022
- **Source**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202120220SB9
- **last_verified**: 2026-05-11

## Summary
Permits ministerial approval of (a) up to 2 units on a single-family-zoned lot AND (b) a one-time urban lot split that divides the parent lot into two lots, each of which may host up to 2 units. Maximum yield: **4 units total** on a single former R-1 parcel.

## Eligibility Checklist

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Zone**: Lot is in a single-family residential zone (R-1, R-1-A, RS, etc.)
- [ ] **Location**: Lot is within an "urbanized area" or "urban cluster" per US Census
- [ ] **Not in Fire Hazard Zone**: Not in a State Responsibility Area (SRA) or Very High Fire Hazard Severity Zone (VHFHSZ), unless mitigation requirements met
- [ ] **Not in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area** (or regulatory floodway), unless mitigation met
- [ ] **Not on Prime Farmland**, Farmland of Statewide Importance, or Unique Farmland (per CA DOC FMMP)
- [ ] **Not in earthquake fault zone** (Alquist-Priolo)
- [ ] **Not on Hazardous Waste Site** (Cortese list / DTSC)
- [ ] **Not in conservation easement or habitat conservation area**
- [ ] **Not historic landmark / not in historic district**
- [ ] **Lot size for split**: parent lot ≥ 2,400 sqft (so each new lot can be ≥ 1,200 sqft)

### [VERIFY] (not auto-checkable from GIS)
- [ ] **Rental history**: Property has NOT been occupied by a tenant in the past 3 years (no demolition/alteration of tenant-occupied housing in last 3 years)
- [ ] **Owner-occupancy commitment**: Applicant affirms intent to occupy one unit as principal residence for ≥3 years from approval of lot split (waived for community land trusts and qualified nonprofits)
- [ ] **No rent-restricted (deed-restricted affordable) housing on parcel**
- [ ] **No Ellis Act withdrawal** in past 15 years
- [ ] **Local ordinance**: Verify city's local SB-9 implementing ordinance (may impose objective design standards)

## Yield Math
- **Two-unit project only** (no lot split): up to 2 units (e.g., duplex or 1 + ADU permitted by SB-9 separately from State ADU law)
- **Two-unit + urban lot split**: up to 2 units × 2 lots = **4 units**
- Local must allow minimum 800 sqft per unit and 4 ft side/rear setbacks
- Lot split requires roughly equal lots (no smaller than 40% of parent area)

## Caveats
- Only one lot split per original parcel — cannot be applied recursively to either resulting lot
- Local agencies CAN impose objective design standards but CANNOT make the project "physically infeasible"
- Local agencies CANNOT require parking beyond 1 space per unit (and zero parking if within 0.5 mi of major transit stop or in a car-share area)
- Some cities have attempted creative restrictions (large minimum lot widths, etc.) — verify local implementing ordinance
- SB-9 stacks with State ADU law in some configurations but not all — needs careful local analysis

## Local Implementation
Most CA cities have adopted local SB-9 ordinances by now. Always check city's ordinance for:
- Objective design standards (form, articulation, materials)
- Specific setback/height/coverage rules
- Owner-occupancy enforcement mechanism
- Application fees and process
