# SB-684 — Small-Lot SF Subdivision (Starter Home Revitalization Act)

- **Bill**: SB 684 (Caballero, 2023)
- **Code sections**: Gov Code §66499.40 et seq. (Subdivision Map Act amendments)
- **Effective**: July 1, 2024
- **Source**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240SB684
- **last_verified**: 2026-05-11

## Summary
Ministerial small-lot subdivision pathway. Allows division of a multi-family zoned parcel (or qualifying urban infill site) up to 5 acres into up to **10 small lots**, each ≥600 sqft, each capable of holding one single-family home. Designed to enable "starter home" for-sale product on infill sites.

## Eligibility Checklist

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Zone**: Parcel is zoned multi-family residential OR is a qualifying urban infill site
- [ ] **Size**: Parcel is ≤ 5 acres
- [ ] **Location**: Parcel is within an urbanized area or urban cluster
- [ ] **Not in Fire Hazard Zone** (SRA / VHFHSZ) unless mitigation met
- [ ] **Not in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area** unless mitigation met
- [ ] **Not on Prime Farmland**, Farmland of Statewide Importance, or Unique Farmland
- [ ] **Not in earthquake fault zone** (Alquist-Priolo)
- [ ] **Not on Hazardous Waste Site**
- [ ] **Not historic landmark / not in historic district**
- [ ] **Not in coastal zone** (unless in qualifying urbanized portion)
- [ ] **Surrounded by qualifying urban uses** (≥75% of perimeter within 1/4 mile is developed with urban uses) — for "qualifying infill" pathway

### [VERIFY]
- [ ] **Site has not been used for housing demolition** that displaced rent-restricted, tenant-occupied, or Ellis-Act-withdrawn housing in past 10 years
- [ ] **Not subject to deed restriction limiting subdivision**
- [ ] **Subdivider commits to ministerial design standards** in local ordinance
- [ ] **Local SB-684 implementing ordinance** — verify city has adopted one (some still in process)
- [ ] **Prevailing wage requirements**: SB-684 generally does not require prevailing wage, but local ordinance may impose

## Yield Math
- Up to **10 lots** per parcel (statutory ceiling)
- Minimum lot size: **600 sqft** per lot
- Each lot may hold one single-family dwelling
- Total yield: **up to 10 SF units**
- Local cannot require minimum dwelling size > 1,750 sqft
- Local cannot require lot dimensions that prevent achieving 10 lots
- **Stacks with AB-130 remainder parcel** — see ab130.md for retaining existing improvements outside the 10-lot count

### Density floor (NOT a cap)
- For projects on **Housing Element sites inventory**: must achieve at least **100%** of max allowable residential density per Housing Element commitment
- For projects on **non-HE sites** (SB-1123 amendment): must achieve at least **66%** of the GREATER of:
  - (i) 66% of max GP density, OR
  - (ii) 66% of applicable density per Gov Code §65583.2(c)(3)(B) (30 du/ac metro, 20 du/ac suburban large, 15 du/ac suburban small, 10 du/ac rural)
- **The GP density is a FLOOR, not a ceiling** — local cannot reject the project for exceeding GP density. The 10-unit cap is the only operative ceiling.
- See research brief: [`robs-business-venture/research/briefs/2026-05-11-sb684-sb1123-gp-density-preemption.md`](../../../../robs-business-venture/research/briefs/2026-05-11-sb684-sb1123-gp-density-preemption.md) for full analysis

## Caveats
- "Qualifying infill" definition is technical — requires careful site mapping for the 75% perimeter test
- The 10-lot cap is per parent parcel — cannot be circumvented by phasing
- For-sale orientation: lots can be sold individually (the bill's "starter home" intent)
- Density (units/acre) implied: 10 units on 5 acres = 2 du/ac minimum; on smaller infill sites the effective density is much higher
- Combines well with State ADU law: an ADU may be allowed on each new small lot (verify local)

## Local Implementation
SB-684 took effect July 2024 — many cities are still drafting implementing ordinances. Check:
- Has the city adopted local SB-684 procedures?
- Are objective design standards in place?
- Is there an established processing track or fee schedule?
- Some cities are folding SB-684 into existing planned unit development (PUD) or small-lot subdivision codes
