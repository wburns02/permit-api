# State ADU Law

- **Code sections**: Gov Code §65852.2 (ADU) and §65852.22 (JADU)
- **Statute current as of**: 2026-05-11 (incorporating SB-1211 update)
- **Source**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/codes_displaySection.xhtml?sectionNum=65852.2&lawCode=GOV
- **last_verified**: 2026-05-11

## Summary
Ministerial, by-right approval of accessory dwelling units (ADUs) and junior accessory dwelling units (JADUs) on most residentially-zoned parcels. Strong state preemption of restrictive local rules; 60-day approval shot clock; cannot require replacement parking; minimum allowable sizes preempt restrictive local minimums.

## SFR Lot Rules

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Zone**: Parcel is in a single-family or multi-family residential zone (or mixed-use permitting residential)
- [ ] **Has existing or proposed primary dwelling**: ADU is "accessory" — needs a primary

### [VERIFY]
- [ ] **Local ADU ordinance**: Verify city's ADU ordinance for objective design standards (city must have one current with state law)
- [ ] **Setback compliance**: 4 ft minimum side/rear for new construction; 0 ft for conversion of existing structures
- [ ] **Utilities at the lot**: Connections may be required

### SFR Yield
- Existing primary unit (1)
- **PLUS 1 detached or attached ADU** up to 1,200 sqft (state minimum local must allow: 800 sqft)
- **PLUS 1 Junior ADU (JADU)** up to 500 sqft, within existing primary structure with shared entry permitted
- **SFR total**: existing + 1 ADU + 1 JADU = up to 3 units on a single-family lot

## Multi-Family Lot Rules

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Zone**: MF zone with existing residential structure

### [VERIFY]
- [ ] **Existing unit count**

### MF Yield
- Existing units (unchanged)
- **PLUS** conversion ADUs within existing non-livable space: at least 1 per 4 existing units OR 25% of existing unit count (whichever is greater)
- **PLUS up to 8 detached ADUs** (per SB-1211; was 2 pre-2025) — see [[sb1211]]

## Universal Rules (apply everywhere)
- **No replacement parking** required when garage/parking is converted to ADU
- **No off-street parking** required if ADU is within 0.5 mi of public transit, in historic district, in permit-required parking zone, or in car-share zone
- **60-day approval shot clock** for ministerial review (90 days if discretionary, though state generally requires ministerial)
- **No owner-occupancy requirement** for ADUs permitted between 2020-2025; varies for JADUs (some local owner-occupancy enforcement)
- **No HOA restrictions** that prohibit ADUs (state preempts)
- **Sale of ADUs**: state allows separate sale of ADU under specific conditions (qualifying nonprofit / community land trust)

## Caveats
- An ADU and a JADU on the same SFR lot is permitted (1 ADU + 1 JADU = 2 accessory units)
- ADU permitted under State ADU law stacks with SB-9 in some configurations — city interpretation varies; generally SB-9 units count as primary residential, not ADUs
- Recent legislation has tightened state preemption; older local ADU ordinances may be unenforceable

## Local Implementation
- Verify city has a current ADU ordinance (cities are required to update regularly to match state law)
- Check for objective design standards (height, materials, articulation)
- Confirm fee schedule
