# SB-1123 — Extension of SB-684 to Vacant SF-Zoned Lots

- **Bill**: SB 1123 (Caballero, 2024)
- **Code sections**: Amends Gov Code §66499.40 et seq. (extends SB-684)
- **Effective**: January 1, 2025
- **Source**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240SB1123
- **last_verified**: 2026-05-11

## Summary
Amends SB-684 to extend the ministerial small-lot subdivision pathway to **vacant single-family-zoned lots** (which were previously excluded — SB-684 covered only MF zones + qualifying infill). Same 10-lot, 600-sqft minimum standard. Designed to unlock vacant infill SFR-zoned land for "starter home" subdivision.

## Eligibility Checklist

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Zone**: Parcel is in a single-family residential zone
- [ ] **Size**: Parcel is ≤ 5 acres
- [ ] **Location**: Parcel is within an urbanized area or urban cluster (US Census)
- [ ] **Not in Fire Hazard Zone** (SRA / VHFHSZ) unless mitigation met
- [ ] **Not in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area** unless mitigation met
- [ ] **Not on Prime Farmland**, Farmland of Statewide Importance, or Unique Farmland
- [ ] **Not in earthquake fault zone** (Alquist-Priolo)
- [ ] **Not on Hazardous Waste Site**
- [ ] **Not historic landmark / not in historic district**
- [ ] **Not in coastal zone** (unless in qualifying urbanized portion)
- [ ] **Vacancy indicator**: Improvement value = $0 OR no building footprint on parcel (strong signal of vacant; not definitive)

### [VERIFY]
- [ ] **Truly vacant on the ground** — improvement value of $0 in assessor data is suggestive, not definitive. Inspect or check recent satellite imagery. Sheds, slabs, or partial improvements may disqualify.
- [ ] **No housing demolition in past 10 years** that displaced rent-restricted, tenant-occupied, or Ellis-Act-withdrawn housing
- [ ] **Not subject to deed restriction limiting subdivision**
- [ ] **Local SB-684/1123 implementing ordinance** — confirm city has adopted updated procedures

## Yield Math
Identical to SB-684:
- Up to **10 lots** per parcel (statutory ceiling)
- Minimum lot size: **600 sqft** per lot
- Each lot may hold one single-family dwelling
- Total yield: **up to 10 SF units**
- Stacks with AB-130 remainder parcel option — but **note**: AB-130 remainder cannot have new residential, so on a truly vacant lot the remainder play is typically about preserving non-residential structures (sheds, agricultural improvements, infrastructure) or designating remainder for open space / infrastructure

### Density floor (NOT a cap) — see [[sb684]]
- SB-1123 reduced the SB-684 density requirement from 100% to **66%** of max allowable density for projects on **non-Housing-Element sites**
- Floor is the greater of (i) 66% × GP density or (ii) 66% × §65583.2 applicable density (30/20/15/10 du/ac by tier)
- **GP density is a FLOOR, not a cap** — 10-unit statutory ceiling is the operative limit
- See research brief: [`robs-business-venture/research/briefs/2026-05-11-sb684-sb1123-gp-density-preemption.md`](../../../../robs-business-venture/research/briefs/2026-05-11-sb684-sb1123-gp-density-preemption.md)

## Caveats
- "Vacant" is the key gate — and it's not a GIS field. Always flag for ground verification.
- Recently demolished parcels (within 10 years of tenant-occupied demolition) are excluded
- Some SFR zones in some cities have minimum lot widths that may complicate fitting 10 lots — verify dimensional feasibility
- Cities are still developing local procedures; some may be unfamiliar with SB-1123 specifically (as a 2024 amendment to SB-684)

## Local Implementation
Most cities are folding SB-1123 into their SB-684 procedures. Specifically check:
- Has the city updated its SB-684 ordinance to acknowledge the vacant SFR pathway?
- Are there additional standards for vacant-lot subdivisions vs. MF-zone subdivisions?
