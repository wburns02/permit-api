# AB-130 — Remainder Parcel Modifier for SB-684/SB-1123

- **Bill**: AB 130 (2025 housing budget trailer bill)
- **Code sections**: Amends Subdivision Map Act / Starter Home Revitalization Act provisions
- **Effective**: 2025 (verify exact effective date against chaptered text)
- **Source**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB130 (verify)
- **last_verified**: 2026-05-11 — **near training cutoff; verify against current statute before relying**

## Summary
AB 130 creates a **"remainder parcel"** carve-out for SB-684/SB-1123 small-lot subdivisions. Allows a developer to subdivide up to 10 new residential lots **plus** retain a separate "remainder parcel" that does **NOT** count toward the 10-lot statutory limit, provided the remainder contains no new residential units.

This unlocks parcels where preserving an existing structure (legacy SFR house, agricultural building, infrastructure improvement) is preferable to demolition.

## How It Works

```
Before AB-130:
  Parcel must be fully subdivided into ≤10 lots
  Existing house = either one of the 10 lots OR must be demolished

After AB-130:
  Parcel splits into [10 new small lots] + [1 remainder parcel]
  Remainder houses the existing structure
  Existing structure does not count against the 10-lot cap
```

## Eligibility Checklist

### GIS-checkable
- [ ] **Project qualifies for SB-684 or SB-1123** — see [[sb684]] / [[sb1123]] for prerequisites
- [ ] **Existing improvement on parcel**: improvement value > $0 in assessor data (suggests existing structure to preserve)
- [ ] **Lot has sufficient area** to support 10 new ≥600 sqft lots + remainder

### [VERIFY]
- [ ] **Existing structure is "legal" / permitted** (not a code violation)
- [ ] **Existing structure to remain** (developer must commit to preservation, not demolition)
- [ ] **Remainder parcel does not contain new residential units** (modifier requirement)
- [ ] **Remainder uses are limited**: existing structures, open space, infrastructure serving the new units, or other non-residential
- [ ] **Sale / lease / financing restrictions** on remainder parcel comply with statute (generally restricted unless meets specific criteria such as preserved legal structure or designated open space)
- [ ] **Local SB-684 procedure accommodates remainder parcel** designations
- [ ] **Verify against chaptered statute text** — AB 130 is recent; details may have been amended

## Yield Math

```
sb684_sb1123_yield = up to 10 new SF units (per parent statute)
ab130_modifier      = +1 retained existing structure (NOT counted toward 10)
                       OR +0 if remainder is open space / infrastructure only
                       
total_units_built  = 10 new + (1 retained if applicable) = 11 total dwellings
                     (with the retained unit on a separate, restricted-sale lot)
```

## Caveats

- **Remainder cannot host NEW residential units** — its purpose is preservation, infrastructure, or open space
- **Sale/lease/financing restrictions** apply to remainder parcel — designed to prevent abuse (i.e., backdoor subdivision into 11 freely-tradable lots)
- AB 130 is a **modifier**, not a standalone state-law qualifier — parcel must independently qualify for SB-684 or SB-1123 first
- **Statutory recency**: 2025 budget trailer bill is near the training cutoff. Verify chaptered text and any subsequent amendments before encoding final yield in a memo
- Cities may not have updated local procedures yet to accommodate remainder parcel designations — first applications may face procedural friction

## Strategic Use Cases

1. **Acquired SFR parcel with existing house worth preserving** (good condition, rental income, neighborhood compatibility)
   - Subdivide 10 new starter homes on the rear / side portion
   - Keep existing house on remainder
   - Preserves $300-600k of existing improvement value
   
2. **Infill parcel with non-residential infrastructure** (well, storage building, ag improvement)
   - Subdivide residential portion under SB-684/1123
   - Retain ag/infrastructure on remainder
   - No demolition cost
   
3. **Mitigates neighborhood opposition**
   - Preserves a visible streetscape element (a mature house, mature landscaping)
   - Reduces "tear-down" optics that drive neighborhood pushback
